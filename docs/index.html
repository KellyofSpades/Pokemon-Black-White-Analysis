Pokémon Black and White: Translation Analysis

Synopsis

Pokémon Black and White are the fifth generation entries into the Pokémon series. Upon initial release, the game recieved backlash for being too restrictive, only allowing the player to catch Pokémon from that region until the post-game, having similar Pokémon to previous generations or lazier designs, and the most heavily criticized being too linear and story driven.

But now, Pokémon Black and WHite are considered some of the best games in the entire series, so what made everyone suddenly change thier minds? The story and overall interactions between the character. Many factors lead up to a good story with interactions, however, the story or overall game can be vastly different when translated from its native language. In Pokémon's case, Japanese is the original language, so translating to English has little to no differences, but what about other languages? The purpose of this is to see how translating the game from an already translated English to other languages, such as German and Arabic, handles, does it read the same? What differences were made when being translated a second time? And is the story and interactions still the same?
